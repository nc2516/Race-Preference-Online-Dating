How does racial preference and discrimination play into online dating apps?

By: Nina Chu

There are many factors that play into peopleâ€™s preferences and biases, but the reality is discrimination is disguised in the idea of preferences. Datings apps have been able to reveal inherit discrimination even if there are no race filters by seeing who swipes left (no) on who, and compiling that data to reveal patterns. While the popular dating app among gay men, Grindr, has chosen to remove race filters following the Black Lives Matter movement, other apps have chosen to keep their race filters, saying it benefits minorities who do not want to be fetishized. However, whether or not there are race filters may not matter, since roots of bias run deep into our subconscious.

In a study conducted by Wei-Chin Hwang, 2,832 dating profiles from LA were chosen at random from a dating app to be sampled. Four racial groups were chosen (White, Black, Latino, and Asian). 354 male and females for each racial group, who were looking for heterosexual relationships.

In order to generalize findings to individuals of one race, the relatively small number of data from mixed-race individuals was excluded from the analysis. Which resulted in 2,123 collected (523 Asians, 504 Blacks, 473 Latinos, and 632 Whites).

Percent Willing to Date by Race and Gender

Whites: Results indicated that White men (97.1 %) and women (98.7 %) were highly willing to intra-racially date. White women were less willing than White men to date inter-racially. White men (77.1 %) were more willing than White women (42.3 %)to date Asians as well as Latinos (76.8 and 50.8 %, respectively) White men (48.3 %) and women (36.3 %) were similarly low, but still evidenced gender differences, in their willingness to date Blacks.

Blacks: Results indicated that Blacks were highly willing to date intraracially, with a slightly higher but significant percentage of Black women (96.4 %) more willing to date Black men than vice versa (91.8 %). Regarding willingness to date other groups, Black men were more willing than Black women to out-date. Willingness to date Asians was 78.4 and 47.8 % , willingness to date Latinos was 80.0 and 52.2 %, and willingness to date Whites was 73.3 and 57.4 %, respectively.

Latinos: A slightly higher but significant percentage of Latinos (98.7 %) were more willing to date Latinas than vice versa (92.5 %). Latinos (71.2 %) were more willing than Latinas (42.1 %) to date Asians, but were similar low (45.1 and 42.5 %, respectively) in their willingness to date Blacks, and similarly high (88.4 and 84.6 %, respectively) in their willingness to date Whites.

Asians: Although 97.3 % of Asian men were willing to date Asian women, only 77.8 % of Asian women were willing to date Asian men. Regarding willingness to date inter-racially, Asian men (38.5 %) and women (32.0 %) were similarly low in their willingness to date Blacks. Asian men (66.7 %) were more willing than Asian women (45.9 %) to date Latinos, and Asian women (91.7 %) were more willing than Asian men (75.7 %) to date Whites.
